Both the unit circle trigonometry and the 3D-to-2D projection rely on the same geometric principle: ratios of lengths relative to a reference distance.

In both cases, the actual coordinates are normalized by a reference measure:
 + Circle: normalized by radius r.
 + Projection: normalized by depth z.

This normalization produces dimensionless ratios (sine, cosine, or projected coordinates) that describe geometry in a simplified way.

Aspect Ratio 

AR = width/height

x' = x/(z*AR)
y' = y/z

If AR > 1 (wide screen), x' is compressed horizontally.
If AR < 1 (tall screen), x' is stretched horizontally.
